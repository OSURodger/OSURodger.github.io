Entry date 2: 4/26/2021

Going into this assignment, I was basically completely blind as to how anything worked apart from the most basic concepts of programming. That is to say, brackets, semicolons, ect. I feel like I may have been brute-forcing several elements of the CSS to make it look like the mock-up. Particularly, when making the nav-bar, and when positioning the twit icon.

I couldn’t get the text in the title or in the twit content to have the correct weight as shown in the mocks. It seems that there are only three different weights for the font, and I needed one between normal and light. I didn’t find a solution to these problems really, but I think the issue with font size might actually be my screen resolution.

Primarily, while doing the assignment, I used the lecture notes and w3schools to look up how to do things and what the proper formatting was.

I had to google how to make the button get slightly bigger on hover. I first tried to just increase the size of the button on hover, but that didn’t work because the button would end up growing in the north-west direction. That is how I learned about the scale function. Lastly, the positioning of the search-bar itself was a lot of googling, and my solution was probably not as simple as it could have been if I had just tried to use the “position” feature.
